About Warner Bros.

Our Story

Warner Bros. has a rich legacy that spans nearly a century of iconic entertainment. It all began in 1923 when four brothers – Harry, Albert, Sam, and Jack Warner – founded Warner Bros. Studios. The scrappy pioneers revolutionized the film industry with innovations like synchronized sound in The Jazz Singer (1927), the first-ever "talkie."

Over the decades, Warner Bros. has been home to timeless characters like Bugs Bunny, Batman, Wonder Woman, and Harry Potter. The studio has produced groundbreaking films, from classics like Casablanca to modern blockbusters like The Dark Knight trilogy. Today, Warner Bros. continues to push boundaries across film, television, animation, video games, and the burgeoning world of interactive entertainment.

Fun Facts

  • Did you know? The iconic Warner Bros. water tower isn't just a set piece! It's a functioning water tower that still serves the studio lot.
  • Mel Blanc, the "Man of a Thousand Voices" responsible for voicing Bugs Bunny, Daffy Duck, and countless Looney Tunes characters, is buried in Hollywood Forever Cemetery with the epitaph: "That's All Folks!"
  • Warner Bros. wasn't always "Warner Bros." The brothers' original surname was "Wonskolaser."
